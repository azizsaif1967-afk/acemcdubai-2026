Culture transformation: the 18-month programme.

By Dr. Aftab Ahmed · 15 Sep 2024
← Back to all insights

Culture doesn't change because of a poster. It changes because of a thousand small decisions, made consistently, over a long enough period that people forget the old way felt normal. Eighteen months is the minimum honest answer to "how long does culture change take." Anything shorter is usually a communications campaign, not a culture transformation.

The six stages

  1. Diagnose — interviews, observation, survey. Understand the culture as it is, not as the leadership imagines it.
  2. Decide — what specifically must change, stated in behavioural terms, not values words.
  3. Design — the mechanisms: hiring, promotion, performance, recognition, rituals.
  4. Demonstrate — leadership visibly models the change first. Without this, the rest is noise.
  5. Diffuse — equip managers, then team leaders, then frontline.
  6. Defend — sustain the new normal through the next reorganisation, the next leadership change, the next crisis.
“Culture is what happens on the days no one is watching.”

What doesn't work

  • Values posters without mechanism changes.
  • Offsites without follow-through.
  • External facilitators without internal sponsors.
  • "Culture champions" without authority or time.

What does

  • Changing who gets hired, who gets promoted, and who gets let go.
  • Aligning the performance system with the stated behaviours.
  • Weekly leadership check-ins where the culture is reviewed like a commercial KPI.
  • Patience — and the willingness to let the programme survive a quarter of underwhelming results.
◆ Key Takeaways
  • Eighteen months is the minimum.
  • Behaviours, not values words.
  • Change the mechanisms — hiring, promotion, performance.
  • Leadership models first, or nothing changes.

Culture work is the most important work a leadership team does. It is also the easiest to fake. The organisations that succeed are the ones where the CEO is willing to be the first employee whose behaviour changes.

Culture transformation, done honestly.
