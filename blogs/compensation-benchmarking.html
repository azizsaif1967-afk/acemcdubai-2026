Compensation benchmarking in the UAE.

By Dr. Aftab Ahmed · 21 Jan 2025
← Back to all insights

Most UAE employers benchmark pay exactly once — when they set up. After that, salaries drift: individual offers, ad-hoc raises, retention counters, management favours. Within a few years, the internal logic of the pay system is gone and everyone knows it. Good benchmarking rebuilds the logic.

What benchmarking is actually for

A pay structure serves three masters: the market (so you can hire), the CFO (so you can budget), and the employee (so they trust the answer to "why am I paid this"). A good benchmark reconciles all three.

The five steps

  1. Job architecture — every role mapped to a job family and a grade. Without this, benchmarking is noise.
  2. Market data — credible published surveys, triangulated with two or three sources. The market moves; last year's numbers lie.
  3. Competitive positioning — decide whether you pay at the market median (P50), upper quartile (P75), or a mix by role family.
  4. Band construction — min, mid and max for each grade, with overlap between adjacent bands.
  5. Internal equity check — a line-by-line review of where real people sit within the bands, flagging outliers in either direction.
“Pay transparency is not a slogan. It is the discipline of being able to answer 'why' without flinching.”

The remediation budget

Most benchmarking projects reveal a remediation cost — people paid meaningfully below the band. Address it in one structured pass, not by individual negotiation. A one-off remediation is cheaper than the drip of attrition that slow drift produces.

Variable pay, bonus and long-term incentives

Base is the most stable layer. Variable pay is where most employers either under-invest (losing sales people) or over-design (creating gaming). Keep bonus schemes simple: two or three objectives, weighted, with a line-of-sight to individual actions. Long-term incentives — equity, deferred cash — are worth considering once you cross 50 employees.

Communicating the system

A pay system nobody understands is a pay system nobody trusts. Build a simple one-page employee explainer: what the bands mean, how grades are set, when pay reviews happen, how variable pay works. Share it. Repeat it.

◆ Key Takeaways
  • Benchmarking without job architecture is noise.
  • Pick a competitive position deliberately — P50, P75, or mixed.
  • Address remediation in one structured pass, not ad hoc.
  • Publish a one-page explainer. Ambiguity is expensive.

A good pay structure is boring. It doesn't need to be defended, because it makes sense on its own. Getting there takes a structured project — but the benefit compounds for years.

Compensation benchmarking project.

Eight to ten weeks: job architecture, market data, bands, equity check, remediation plan, employee communication.
