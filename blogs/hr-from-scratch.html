Building HR from scratch: the 90-day SME plan.

By Dr. Aftab Ahmed · 03 Apr 2025
← Back to all insights

Most founders wait too long. By the time they call us, they have 40 employees, three handwritten contracts, one office manager doing "HR stuff" on the side, and a growing sense that something is quietly broken. This is the 90-day plan we run when a UAE founder finally says: we need HR.

Days 1–14: diagnose, don't design

The biggest mistake is to start drafting policies. Start by reading what's already happening. Pull every signed contract. Map every salary, every variable, every allowance. List every promise the founder has made to every employee. Interview five to seven staff at different levels — not about what they want, but about how work currently gets done.

By day 14 you should be able to answer three questions: what are we actually paying people; what are we actually asking people to do; and where is the biggest compliance risk. That diagnosis defines everything that follows.

Days 15–30: lock down compliance

Build the compliance floor before you build anything else. In the UAE that means: MOHRE contracts aligned with the portal; WPS running cleanly; medical insurance in place and current; gratuity accrual funded on the balance sheet; Emirates ID, visa and labour card status reconciled for every person on the payroll.

Nothing here is strategic. All of it is table stakes. Skipping it means any later work will be built on sand.

“Fix payroll first, culture second, policy third. Always.”

Days 31–60: the essential policies

Seven policies carry most of the weight. Don't draft twenty. Draft these, well:

  1. Employee handbook (the cover document).
  2. Code of conduct.
  3. Leave and attendance.
  4. Performance and probation.
  5. Disciplinary and grievance.
  6. Anti-harassment.
  7. Data protection.

Each should be short, specific, written in plain language, and signed off by the founder. Fancy drafting by an external lawyer who has never met the team is the wrong tool here.

Days 61–75: the one thing that makes it real

Hire a first HR person. Not a generalist looking for a quiet life. Someone who has done the work before, ideally in a similar-sized UAE business, who can hold the ring while the founder steps back. Pay for quality — the economics are unforgiving if you don't.

Days 76–90: rhythm, not theatre

Put three recurring rhythms in place: monthly people review with the founder; quarterly compliance check; bi-annual one-to-one cycle between every manager and every direct report. These three rhythms, done honestly, deliver more than any HR technology investment.

◆ Key Takeaways
  • Diagnose before designing. Read what's already happening.
  • Fix compliance first. The rest is built on it.
  • Seven policies, well written, beat twenty drafted at speed.
  • Hire a first HR person who has done it before, not just learned it.
  • Install three rhythms — monthly, quarterly, bi-annual.

Ninety days is enough to go from "we probably need HR" to a functioning, compliant, rhythm-led people function. More than 90 days, usually, is indulgence. Less than 90 days is fantasy. The founders who commit to the middle path rarely regret it.
